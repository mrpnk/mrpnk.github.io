After a group of Knights and Knaves set up their tents, Thomas (the best cook among them) started cooking at the campfire while all the others settle in a circle around the fire, watching him. He noticed that everyone in the circle seemed to know the two people next to him. Thomas did not know anyone except his good friend Richard. To get to know the round a little more he asked a person in the circle, "You and the two, who sit next to you: is there an odd number of knaves in this small set?" The person answered. Thomas asked another randomly chosen person and received the same answer. Whoever he asked, he kept getting this answer. Finally, after he had asked everybody else, he turned the same question to Richard. Surprisingly his answer was different from all the others. Thomas paused for a moment and then asked Richard, "Are you sitting between two knights?", whereupon Richard replied his answer.

Let n be the number of people present. How many are Knights resp. Knaves, and what are Thomas and Richard?
